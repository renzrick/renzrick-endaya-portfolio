"Ghost Mannequin": Arc'teryx Photography Composition

A method of fashion photography that gives off the illusion that a ghost is wearing the specific piece of clothing.

My Role

Lead Photographer, Designer, Editor

For the final project of my Photography Course, I was assigned the task of planning and producing an individual composite image; mainly intended to display my ability to capture a good exposure through a camera as well as my Adobe Photoshop editing skills. Some project management aspects are present as well, as I planned 3 separate photoshoots with the model to assure a satisfying end product.

Brainstorming Process

  • Within this process, I came across the idea of “ghost mannequin” product photography. I personally felt that this would be a visually appealing and rather fun concept to pursue for my project.

  • I wanted to exhibit the “Beta AR” Arc'teryx jacket, as I wanted to further encapsulate the water resistance of the jacket, because of the Gore-Tex fabric that was used to create the jacket.

  • Along the way, I also wanted to further emphasize the Gore-Tex fabric by incorporating the concept of "Splash Photgraphy" as well.
